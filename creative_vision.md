## One Sentence

*You are the last person keeping a light on in a world that stopped watching — and then the dark starts watching back.*

---

## The Promise

This game makes one promise: **you will feel the weight of being the only one who stayed.**

Not the weight of combat. Not the weight of survival mechanics. The weight of *attention* — of choosing where to look when you can't look everywhere, of hearing something in the dark and deciding if answering is courage or a mistake.

---

## Player Fantasy

**Solitude with purpose.**

The player isn't trapped. They chose this. The lighthouse isn't a prison — it's a responsibility. The fantasy is being the kind of person who stays when everyone else leaves, and discovering whether that was wisdom or avoidance.

---

## Emotional Arc

```
CURIOSITY  →  COMPETENCE  →  DOUBT  →  ACCEPTANCE
  Days 1–3      Days 4–6      Days 7–10    Days 11–14
```

| Phase | The player feels... | The game does... |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Curiosity** | "What is this place? Why am I here?" | Teaches systems, establishes space, plants seeds |
| **Competence** | "I've got this. I can keep this light alive." | Rewards mastery, builds routine, creates comfort |
| **Doubt** | "Something is wrong. Can I trust what I'm hearing?" | Breaks patterns, corrupts signals, erodes certainty |
| **Acceptance** | "I understand now — not the mystery, but myself." | Resolves emotional arc, not plot mechanics |

---

## Creative Pillars

### 1. Vigilance

Every action is about paying attention. The game rewards observation and punishes neglect — not with game-overs, but with atmospheric consequences. The lighthouse dims. The signal grows faint. Something in the distance changes.

### 2. Ambiguity

Nothing is fully explained. The signals, the story, the world — the player must interpret, decide, and live with uncertainty. The game trusts the player to sit with not-knowing.

### 3. Solitude as Strength

Being alone is not a punishment. The keeper stayed for a reason. That reason — not the mystery of the signals — is the emotional spine of the game.

## Visual Language

### Palette

| Element | Purpose | Hex |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Deep sea (midnight blue) | The unknown, the vast | `#0D1B2A` |
| Lighthouse beam (warm amber) | Player agency — the light they control | `#E8A838` |
| Cliff stone (weathered grey) | Stability aging, the world's erosion | `#6B717E` |
| Dawn sky (pale rose) | Consequence — the morning after | `#C9A9A6` |
| Corruption (desaturated red) | When signals become untrustworthy | `#8B3A3A` |

### Visual Rules

- **Less is more.** Communicate through absence. Empty rooms speak. Missing logbook pages tell stories.
- **Light is meaning.** When the lighthouse beam flickers, the player should feel it.
- **The sea is never fully visible.** Show the shore, the rocks, the horizon — never what's beneath.
- **No clutter.** Every visual element serves function or atmosphere. Nothing decorative.

## Tone & Voice

### The game sounds like:

A logbook written by someone careful with words, because words are all they have left. Equipment inspection notes that gradually reveal cracks — a crossed-out entry, a date written three times. Signals that sound professional until they don't.

### The game does NOT sound like:

- ❌ Horror narration ("A chill ran down my spine")
- ❌ Literary prose ("The sea whispered secrets to the ancient stones")  
- ❌ Tutorial voice ("You should check the fuel gauge now")

### Vocabulary standard:

| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
| :--- | :--- |
| "The lens needs cleaning" | "You should clean the lens" |
| "Signal received, bearing NE, 0347h" | "A mysterious signal pierces the night" |
| "Dawn. Clear skies. Nothing on the rocks." | "Morning light revealed an empty, haunting sea" |

## Backstory Directive

Agreed with `NARRATIVE` in CONV-2026-002:

> The player knows *that* something happened. They don't know *what*. The first signals provide functional maritime context. The latter signals start referencing the reason the keeper stayed. The logbook confirms a reason exists but never names it.
>
> The player reconstructs the truth through fragment accumulation, not direct revelation.

---

## What This Game Is NOT

| ❌ | Why |
| :--- | :--- |
| Not a horror game | Tension from uncertainty, not fear. No jump scares, no monsters. |
| Not a puzzle game | Signals are interpreted, not solved. No single correct answers. |
| Not a walking simulator | Mechanical actions every cycle. Passivity has consequences. |
| Not a survival game | Resources are limited but the game isn't about staying alive — it's about staying *vigilant*. |
| Not a visual novel | Player actions have mechanical and narrative consequences beyond text choices. |
